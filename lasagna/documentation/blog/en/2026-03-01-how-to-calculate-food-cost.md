# How to Calculate Food Cost for Your Recipe Business

Knowing your exact food cost is the foundation of a profitable food business. Without this number, you can't price correctly, you risk selling at a loss, and you won't notice when ingredient prices creep up.

## What Goes into Food Cost

Food cost has several components:

- **Ingredients** — cost of all ingredients per serving
- **Preparation loss** — trimmings, evaporation, baking shrinkage
- **Packaging** — boxes, bags, ribbons
- **Overhead** — electricity, gas, equipment depreciation (optional)

For home bakers, the first two components are usually enough to start.

## The Formula

```
Food Cost = sum(ingredient_price / purchase_weight × recipe_weight)
           + preparation loss
           + packaging cost
```

## Example: Chocolate Cake (8 servings)

| Ingredient | Price per kg | Recipe weight | Cost   |
|------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Flour      | $1.20       | 400 g         | $0.48  |
| Butter     | $8.00       | 300 g         | $2.40  |
| Eggs       | $0.30 each  | 4 pcs         | $1.20  |
| Milk       | $1.00/L     | 500 ml        | $0.50  |
| Sugar      | $0.90       | 200 g         | $0.18  |
| Cocoa      | $12.00      | 100 g         | $1.20  |

**Total ingredients:** $5.96 → **per serving: $0.75**

Add packaging (box): $2.00 per cake → $0.25/serving

**Total food cost per serving: ~$1.00**

## How to Set Your Price

A typical markup in the bakery business is 200–400%. This covers your time, overhead, and profit.

With a food cost of $1.00, a recommended price is **$3.00–$5.00 per serving**.
